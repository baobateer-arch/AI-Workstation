# Product Hunt Launch Package

## Product name

AI Workstation

## Tagline

Turn Kindle into an AI Agent Command Center

## Short description

Monitor Claude Code, OpenAI Codex, MiMo Code, quota, and AI balances on a calm, low-power Kindle e-ink dashboard.

## Launch post

Hi Product Hunt 👋

I built AI Workstation after a simple frustration: once I started running multiple coding agents, I spent too much time checking terminals to see whether they were working, waiting for approval, or already stopped.

AI Workstation turns an old Kindle into an always-visible command center for:

- Claude Code Monitor
- Codex Monitor
- MiMo Monitor
- AI Resource Tracking
- Kindle E-Ink Dashboard

The dashboard is intentionally calm. It does not reproduce logs or add another notification feed. It surfaces the few states that change human action: RUNNING, IDLE, WAITING_APPROVAL, and STOPPED.

The project is open source and designed for local workflows. For people who want help getting a working device, I also offer Personal Setup and custom AI workflow services.

I would love feedback from developers who run two or more coding agents: what information deserves a permanent place on your desk?

## Maker comment

The hardest product decision was not what to add, but what to remove. E-ink forced the dashboard to focus on durable state and human intervention. I hope AI Workstation feels more like an instrument panel than another app demanding attention.
